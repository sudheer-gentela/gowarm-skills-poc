# Talk Track Template

The talk track is what the rep **says live during the call**. It must be usable verbatim or near-verbatim. If any part of the talk track would embarrass the rep when said out loud to a prospect, it's wrong.

## Hard rule: don't leak the system

The rep's talk track must never expose that the CRM data is thin or that they're relying on an AI-generated prep. Avoid phrases like:

- ❌ "I see a note in our system about..."
- ❌ "Looking at our records..."
- ❌ "My CRM says..."
- ❌ "I don't have much context on how this started..."
- ❌ "Let me pull up what I have here..."

If the rep has context, state it as something they *know* ("I understand you're the Director of IT Ops, and James is your CIO"). If they don't have context, don't fake certainty — but pivot to questions rather than admitting the gap.

## 1. Opening (target: 30 seconds of rep talking)

Goal: set expectations, show you've done your homework (without over-claiming), get permission to guide the call.

**Adaptive opening based on context:**

**Stalled / ignored-follow-up scenario** (e.g. post-demo silence, long time-in-stage, multiple unanswered touches):
Write a direct opening that names the reality respectfully. The rep is acknowledging an awkward dynamic, not attacking. Example shape:

> "Thanks for making time. I want to be direct with you — we [last concrete interaction] and I haven't heard back since. I don't want to keep guessing what that silence means. I'd rather have an honest conversation about where this actually stands."

**Early-stage or thin-context scenario:**
Warm, low-pressure, no admission of data gaps. Focus on the rep's intent, not on what they know or don't know.

> "Thanks for making time today. My goal for the next 20 minutes is to understand what's happening on your end, figure out whether what we do is a fit, and if it is, talk about what a useful next step would look like. Does that work? Anything specific you want to make sure we cover?"

**Inbound demo-request scenario:**
Acknowledge they initiated, then pivot to context:

> "Thanks for reaching out. Before I jump into what we do, I'd love to understand what made you fill out the form — what you were hoping to see or learn."

When in doubt, default to warmth over confrontation.

## 2. Context confirmation (target: 2 minutes of conversation, mostly rep listening)

Goal: confirm what you already know confidently rather than asking them to re-explain from scratch. Build credibility (you paid attention) without overreaching.

**If you have real context to confirm:**

State what you know as facts the rep owns, not as notes from a system:

> "Before I ask you questions, let me make sure I have the picture right. You're [title] at [company], you're working on [specific project/initiative from payload], and the last time we connected it sounded like [specific topic]. Does that match how you'd describe it, or has anything changed?"

**If context is thin:**

Don't fake a summary. Pivot to curiosity:

> "I don't want to assume I have the full picture. Can you give me the quick version of how you ended up on a call today — what prompted you to look at something like this?"

Either version should be 2-3 sentences the rep says, then shuts up and listens.

## 3. Discovery questions (the 6-8 selected)

Present as a flowing conversation, not an interrogation. For each question, provide:

- The question itself (open-ended)
- A parenthetical "(listening for: ...)" note — this is for the rep's eye only, not said aloud

Example format:

> "Walk me through what a bad week looks like for your team right now." (listening for: pain specificity, who owns the pain, whether pain has a dollar value attached)

Order: broader → more specific. Start with pain/context, move to process/stakeholders, end with timeline/budget if earned.

## 4. Close (target: 1 minute)

Goal: lock in a clear, specific next step. No ambiguity. Never end with "I'll follow up."

**Structure:**

- Summarize what you heard in 2-3 sentences: "So what I'm hearing is..."
- State a recommendation: "Based on that, I think the right next step is [X]."
- Propose a specific `[day/time]`: "Does [day] at [time] work to [specific next action]?"

**For stalled/questionable deals,** offer the rep a disqualify path too:

> "Based on what we've talked through, it sounds like [this isn't a priority right now / the timing's wrong / you're still early]. I can either [specific forward step if signal was good], or we can just agree to reconnect in [timeframe]. Which is more useful for you?"

Giving the prospect a graceful exit is a feature, not a weakness. It respects their time and surfaces real intent.
