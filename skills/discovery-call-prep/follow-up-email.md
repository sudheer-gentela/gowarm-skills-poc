# Follow-up Email Template

Two variants. Both must be under 120 words. Both are drafts the rep will edit post-call based on what was actually said.

## Critical rule: the call hasn't happened yet

When you draft these emails, the call has NOT happened. You are writing templates the rep will customize AFTER the call. This means:

- You do NOT know what the prospect said. Never write "you said X" unless X is verifiable from `interaction_history`.
- You do NOT know what metrics, timelines, or commitments came up on the call.
- You do NOT know the rep's name (the payload doesn't include it).

Use `[bracketed placeholders]` wherever specific post-call content belongs. The rep fills them in before sending. This is a feature, not a flaw.

## Universal rules

- No "I hope this email finds you well."
- No "Thanks for taking the time to chat" (pick one variant where that's appropriate; don't use it in both).
- No em-dashes.
- Subject line: reference the *topic* discussed, not fabricated details. "Re: [topic of the call]" is fine. "The Q2 HIPAA audit pressure" is NOT fine unless HIPAA audit pressure is explicitly in the payload.
- Close with one clear next step. Use `[day/time]` placeholders.
- Sign-off: `[Your name]` or a simple dash. Never invent a name like "Mike" or "Sarah."

## Variant A: Strong signal

Use when: the call went well — clear pain articulated, buyer confirmed or identifiable, timeline named, next step accepted.

Shape:
- Sentence 1: reference the *topic* of the most important thing that came up, using a placeholder for the specific content. Example: "After our conversation today about [the specific pain you described around X], I wanted to get this next step locked in."
- Sentence 2: connect that topic to what you'd show next. No invented percentages or case studies. Example: "I'll put together a walkthrough focused on [the scenario you described]."
- Sentence 3: restate the committed next step with `[day/time]` placeholder.
- Optional: offer a resource as a placeholder: `[attach the relevant case study]`.

### Correct example (payload has minimal detail)

> Subject: Next step after today's call
>
> [First name],
>
> Thanks for the honest conversation today. Based on [the specific pain point you raised] and the timeline we discussed, the right next step is a walkthrough with [the additional stakeholder you mentioned].
>
> Does [day] at [time] work to get that on the calendar?
>
> [Your name]

### Wrong example (this is what to AVOID)

> Subject: The Q2 HIPAA audit pressure — next step
>
> Linda,
>
> Thanks for the honest conversation today. Based on what you shared about the Q2 HIPAA audit pressure and James's mandate to get the CRM situation locked down, it sounds like we're aligned...

Why this is wrong: "Q2 HIPAA audit pressure" and "James's mandate" are invented specifics that weren't in the payload. The rep will look foolish if they send this without those being real. Use placeholders instead.

## Variant B: Weak signal

Use when: the call was flat, non-committal, or ended without a clear next step.

Shape:
- Sentence 1: acknowledge something real about the conversation (topic level, not invented content). Example: "I appreciated you taking the time today to walk through [the general area you discussed]."
- Sentence 2: offer a low-commitment value — ONE of: a suggested resource, a relevant benchmark, an invitation to a future touchpoint. Use a placeholder if the specific resource needs to be chosen post-call.
- Sentence 3: propose a specific lightweight re-engagement with `[day/time]` placeholder. Not an open-ended "let me know when you're ready."

### Correct example

> Subject: Quick follow-up from today
>
> [First name],
>
> Appreciate you making time today. It sounds like [the area we discussed] isn't at the top of your priority list right now, which is useful for me to know.
>
> If things shift, I can send over [a relevant resource based on what came up]. Otherwise, worth a 15-minute check-in in [3-4 weeks]?
>
> [Your name]

## Subject line guidance

Good subjects reference a topic, not fabricated detail:

- ✅ "Next step after today's call"
- ✅ "Quick follow-up from today"
- ✅ "Re: [topic from the call]"
- ❌ "The Q2 HIPAA audit pressure — next step" (invented specificity)
- ❌ "The project delay problem — next step" (invented specificity unless the project-delay detail is in the payload)

When in doubt, choose a generic-but-clean subject over a specific-but-fabricated one.
