# Follow-up Email Template

Two variants. Both must be under 120 words. Both should feel like a human wrote them post-call, not a template.

## Universal rules

- No "I hope this email finds you well." No "Thanks for taking the time to chat." (The second is fine in one variant, avoid both.)
- No em-dashes.
- Subject line: reference something specific from the call, not "Following up" or "Great chatting today."
- Close with one clear next step, specifically dated.
- Use the prospect's first name. Sign off with just the rep's first name.

## Variant A: Strong signal

Use when: clear pain articulated, economic buyer confirmed or identifiable, timeline named, next step accepted.

Shape:
- Sentence 1: reference the most specific thing they said — their own words if possible, paraphrased
- Sentence 2: connect that pain to what you'd show them next (the demo, the technical deep dive, the proposal)
- Sentence 3: restate the committed next step with day/time
- Sentence 4 (optional): a resource or attachment that reinforces the value — a case study, a calculator, a one-pager

Subject example: "The Q2 forecast accuracy problem — next step"

## Variant B: Weak signal

Use when: vague pain, no clear buyer, no timeline, prospect non-committal, or meeting ended without a next step.

Shape:
- Sentence 1: acknowledge something they said (shows you were listening even if the conversation was flat)
- Sentence 2: offer a low-commitment value — a relevant article, a piece of benchmarking data, an invitation to a webinar
- Sentence 3: propose a specific lightweight re-engagement — "Worth a 15-min check-in in 3 weeks?" — not an open-ended "let me know when you're ready"

Do not pretend the call went better than it did. Do not push for a demo they didn't earn. The goal is to stay warm, not to fake momentum.

Subject example: "Something you might find useful on [topic they mentioned]"
