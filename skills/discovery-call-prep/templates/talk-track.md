# Talk Track Template

The talk track is what the rep uses live during the call. It has four parts.

## 1. Opening (target: 30 seconds of rep talking)

Goal: set expectations, get permission to ask questions, show you've done homework.

Shape:
- Acknowledge something specific from the interaction history or their background (not generic pleasantries)
- State the purpose of the call: "understand your situation, see if there's a fit, and if there is, talk about what next steps look like"
- Ask for permission to guide: "Does that work for you? Any specific things you want to make sure we cover?"

Write this as what the rep would actually say, in their voice — conversational, not scripted-sounding.

## 2. Context confirmation (target: 2 minutes)

Goal: confirm what you already know rather than asking them to re-explain from scratch.

Shape:
- "Before I ask you questions, let me make sure I have the picture right — [summarize what you know from the payload]. Does that match how you'd describe it?"
- Let them correct or add color

This builds credibility (you paid attention) and saves the prospect from repeating themselves.

## 3. Discovery questions (the 6-8 you selected)

Present these as a flowing conversation, not an interrogation. For each question, include:
- The question itself (open-ended)
- A very short note in parentheses about what you're listening for — this is for the rep's eye only, not to be said out loud

Example:
> "Walk me through what a bad week looks like for your team right now." (listening for: pain specificity, who owns the pain, whether pain has a dollar value attached)

Order questions from broader → more specific. Start with pain/context, move to process/stakeholders, end with timeline/budget if earned.

## 4. Close (target: 1 minute)

Goal: lock in a clear, specific next step. No ambiguity.

Shape:
- Summarize what you heard in 2-3 sentences — "So what I'm hearing is…"
- State a recommendation: "Based on that, I think the right next step is [X]"
- Propose a specific time: "Does [day] at [time] work to get [Y] on the call as well?"

The close must name a specific next play (demo, bringing in another stakeholder, technical deep dive, etc.) and a specific calendar commitment. Never end with "I'll follow up."
