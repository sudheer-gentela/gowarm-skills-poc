# Prep Brief Template

Target: under 250 words. Plain prose, no bullet points unless absolutely necessary.

## Structure

**The one thing that matters** (1 sentence)
What is the single most important fact or dynamic the rep should walk in knowing? This might be a stated pain, a trigger event, a competitor mention, or a key stakeholder dynamic.

**Who you're talking to** (2-3 sentences)
Prospect name, title, company context. Any signal from their background, tenure, or LinkedIn activity that's relevant. Mention the company only to the extent it shapes the conversation (recent funding, layoffs, leadership change, growth stage).

**Where the deal stands** (2-3 sentences)
How did this prospect enter the pipeline? What's happened so far in the interaction history? What's their engagement level? What's been promised or discussed previously?

**What to listen for** (2-3 sentences)
Given the MEDDPICC gaps, what are the 1-2 most critical things to come out of this call having learned? Examples: "Whether the prospect is the economic buyer or points to someone else." "What metric they're actually measured on this quarter." "Whether there's a real budget or this is exploratory."

**Watch-outs** (1-2 sentences)
Any risks: competitor in play, buying committee size, history of deals stalling at this stage, timing red flags.

## Example tone

Good: "Priya has been in her VP RevOps role for 4 months — she mentioned in her outbound reply that she's been asked to deliver a forecasting overhaul by end of Q2. Lead with that deadline."

Bad: "Priya is a VP of RevOps who is interested in our forecasting solution and will be a good prospect to talk to about our capabilities."

Be specific. Be short. The rep is skimming this in the 5 minutes before the call.
