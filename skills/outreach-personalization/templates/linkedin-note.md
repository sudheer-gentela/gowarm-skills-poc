# LinkedIn Connection Note Template

The LinkedIn note is a different medium from email. Different rules.

## Hard constraints

- **280 characters MAX** (LinkedIn's limit is 300; leave breathing room for the rep to tweak).
- Single paragraph. No line breaks.
- No links. LinkedIn strips them or de-ranks the connection request.
- No email addresses. Same reason.

## What works on LinkedIn that doesn't in email

- First-name-only signoff, no title. The rep's LinkedIn profile shows their title already.
- Slightly warmer opener — LinkedIn is a quasi-personal platform, not the inbox. "Hey Maya" works where "Hey Maya" in email feels overly familiar for a cold touch.
- Direct connection ask. The person decides whether to accept before they read anything else anyway.

## What doesn't work on LinkedIn that might seem to

- Don't repeat the email hook verbatim. If the rep sends both email and LinkedIn, the prospect shouldn't read the same paragraph twice.
- Don't pitch in the note. Connection requests that pitch get ignored — or worse, reported.
- Don't ask for a meeting in the note. Ask to connect, and let the meeting ask come in a follow-up message.

## Structure

**Sentence 1**: Brief reason for connecting — anchored to the same signal as the email but phrased differently.

**Sentence 2** (optional): One-line about what the rep does, only if the hook sentence doesn't imply it.

**Sentence 3**: A soft ask to connect. "Would love to connect" works if it's true.

## Example output shapes

**Hook from prospect's post**:
> Hey Maya — your post on playbook execution being 10% of the work lines up with something we spend a lot of time thinking about. Running GoWarmCRM, which sits on top of Salesforce and surfaces whether reps are actually running the playbook. Would love to connect.

(278 characters)

**Hook from account event**:
> Hey Maya — saw Ferrovia's Series B extension and Devin's move from Formica. Been following you on the sales execution side for a bit. I run GoWarmCRM — we work with VP Sales going through exactly the kind of rebuild you're in now. Open to connecting?

(253 characters)

**Sparse signal (fallback)**:
> Hi Tomás — came across your profile while looking at VP Sales in growth-stage SaaS. Running GoWarmCRM, which sits on top of the CRM and surfaces whether reps are running the playbook. Always up for trading notes with sales leaders. Open to connecting?

(249 characters)

## What must never appear in the note

- Anything from the email body verbatim (makes the two touches redundant).
- Any of the banned phrasings in `outreach-principles.md`.
- Any reference to reactions/likes.
- "Hope this finds you well."
- An attempt to book a meeting in the first-connect note.
- Your company's URL — the prospect will check your profile.
